Why PPC Works for Solicitors

When someone searches "personal injury solicitor Dublin" or "family law solicitor Cork," they need help now. PPC advertising places your firm directly in front of these high-intent searchers the moment they're looking for legal services.

Immediate Visibility Where It Matters

Unlike SEO, which builds results over months, Google Ads gives you top-of-page placement from day one. For new firms, firms entering new practice areas, or solicitors in competitive markets like Dublin, this is often the fastest way to start generating enquiries.

Google Ads appear above organic results for commercial search terms. When a potential client types "solicitor near me" or "conveyancing solicitor Galway," your firm can be the first thing they see. That visibility translates directly into phone calls and contact form submissions.

PPC also gives you full control over your budget. You set a daily spend, choose which areas you serve, and only pay when someone actually clicks through to your website. There are no long-term contracts with Google, and you can pause or adjust campaigns at any time.

High-Intent Clicks, Not Window Shoppers

The real strength of PPC for solicitors is intent. People clicking on Google Ads for legal services are actively looking for help. They are not browsing casually. They have a legal issue, and they want to speak with a solicitor.

  • Target people searching for your exact practice areas and locations
  • Appear only during business hours or when your team can take calls
  • Filter out irrelevant searches with negative keyword management
  • Track every click, call, and form submission back to the ad that generated it
  • Adjust bids based on which keywords actually bring in clients
  • Scale up spend on campaigns that are working, pause those that aren't
Worth noting: Legal search terms are among the most expensive on Google. That's precisely why expert management matters. A poorly run campaign can waste thousands. A well-managed one can be the most profitable marketing channel your firm has.

Our PPC Approach for Law Firms

We don't run generic ad campaigns. Every element is built around legal search behaviour in Ireland, backed by the experience of Juris Digital managing Google Ads for law firms for over 15 years.

1

Legal Keyword Research

We identify the exact terms potential clients use when searching for solicitors in your practice areas and locations. This goes beyond the obvious. We find long-tail variations, question-based queries, and local modifiers that your competitors are missing. We also build extensive negative keyword lists to prevent your budget being wasted on irrelevant clicks.

2

Ad Copy That Converts

Writing ads for solicitors requires care. Your ads need to be persuasive while remaining compliant with LSRA (Legal Services Regulatory Authority) advertising guidelines. We write ad copy that highlights your firm's strengths, includes clear calls to action, and speaks directly to what the searcher needs. Every ad is tested and refined based on real performance data.

3

Dedicated Landing Pages

Sending paid traffic to your homepage is one of the most common mistakes in legal PPC. We create focused landing pages for each campaign, designed to match the searcher's intent and guide them toward contacting your firm. A person searching for "employment law solicitor Limerick" should land on a page about employment law, not your general homepage.

4

Bid Management and Optimisation

Legal keywords are expensive, so every euro needs to count. We monitor bids daily, adjusting for time of day, device type, location, and competition. We use conversion data to shift budget toward the keywords and ads that actually generate client enquiries, not just clicks. This ongoing optimisation is what separates profitable campaigns from money pits.

Frequently Asked Questions About PPC for Solicitors

How much should a solicitor spend on Google Ads? ▼
It depends on your practice areas and location. Personal injury in Dublin is far more competitive than conveyancing in a smaller town. Most solicitor firms we work with spend between €1,500 and €5,000 per month on ad spend, plus management fees. We'll recommend a budget based on your specific goals and the realistic cost-per-click in your market. Starting smaller and scaling up based on results is a perfectly sensible approach.
How quickly will I see results from PPC? ▼
Almost immediately. Unlike SEO, which takes months to build momentum, Google Ads can start sending visitors to your website within hours of launching a campaign. However, the first two to four weeks are typically an optimisation period where we refine targeting, test ad copy, and adjust bids. Most firms see strong, consistent lead flow within the first month.
Should I use PPC or SEO for my law firm? ▼
Ideally, both. PPC gives you immediate visibility and leads while SEO builds your long-term organic presence. Many of our clients start with PPC to generate enquiries right away, then add SEO so that over time their organic rankings reduce their dependence on paid ads. The two channels work well together, and we can manage both for your firm.
Are there LSRA rules around Google Ads for solicitors? ▼
Yes. The Legal Services Regulatory Authority (LSRA) sets advertising guidelines that apply to all solicitor marketing in Ireland, including paid ads. Your ad copy and landing pages need to comply with these rules. We're familiar with the requirements and ensure all campaigns we run meet LSRA standards. This is one of the reasons working with a legal-sector specialist matters.
Will I own my Google Ads account? ▼
Absolutely. Your Google Ads account belongs to you. We set it up under your firm's ownership, and you have full access at all times. If you ever decide to part ways, you keep everything: the account, the data, and all campaign history. We don't believe in holding clients' accounts hostage. That data is yours.
What's the difference between your PPC management and doing it myself? ▼
You can certainly run Google Ads yourself, and Google makes it easy to set up a basic campaign. The problem is that basic campaigns waste money. Legal clicks cost between €5 and €30 each, sometimes more for personal injury terms. Without proper negative keywords, bid strategies, ad testing, and conversion tracking, you'll pay for clicks that never become clients. Our management typically pays for itself by reducing wasted spend and increasing the conversion rate of the traffic you do get.
