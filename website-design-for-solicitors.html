Why Solicitor Websites Are Different

A law firm website isn't like any other business site. It needs to meet regulatory requirements, build immediate trust, and turn visitors into people who pick up the phone. Generic web designers don't understand these demands.

Compliance Comes First

The LSRA (Legal Services Regulatory Authority) sets specific rules about how solicitors can advertise their services in Ireland. Your website is advertising, and every page needs to comply. That includes how you present testimonials, the claims you make about outcomes, and how you describe your fees.

We build every site with LSRA guidelines in mind from the start, not as an afterthought. This protects your firm from regulatory issues and gives potential clients confidence that you operate professionally.

Most generic web design agencies have never heard of the LSRA. That alone should tell you why working with a legal-sector specialist matters.

Trust Signals and Conversion

When someone visits a solicitor's website, they're usually dealing with something stressful: a property purchase, a family dispute, a workplace issue, or an injury. They need to trust you before they'll make contact.

  • Professional design that reflects the seriousness of legal work
  • Clear display of qualifications, Law Society membership, and accreditations
  • Easy-to-find contact details on every page
  • Practice area pages that answer real client questions
  • Fast load times and mobile-friendly layouts
  • Calls to action that make getting in touch simple
The reality: A good-looking website isn't enough. It needs to be built with a clear structure that guides visitors from their first question to contacting your firm. Every design decision we make is informed by data from hundreds of law firm websites, backed by the experience of Juris Digital.

Frequently Asked Questions About Solicitor Web Design

How long does it take to build a solicitor website? ▼
Most solicitor websites take 4 to 6 weeks from kickoff to launch. The timeline depends on how many practice area pages you need, how quickly content is approved, and whether we're writing the copy from scratch. Larger firms with multiple offices and many practice areas may take a bit longer. We'll give you a clear timeline before we start.
Will my website be compliant with LSRA advertising rules? ▼
Yes. We build every solicitor website with LSRA (Legal Services Regulatory Authority) guidelines in mind. This includes how testimonials are displayed, claims about case outcomes, fee transparency, and any other advertising requirements that apply to solicitors in Ireland. We stay up to date with regulatory changes so your site remains compliant.
Do I need to provide the website content myself? ▼
No. We can write all the content for your website, including practice area pages, solicitor bios, and your homepage. We'll ask you some questions about your firm and services, then produce clear, client-focused copy that's also optimised for Google. If you prefer to write your own content, we're happy to work with that too.
Can you redesign my existing solicitor website? ▼
Absolutely. Many of our clients come to us with an existing website that's outdated or underperforming. We'll assess your current site, preserve any SEO value it has, and rebuild it with a modern design, faster performance, and better conversion elements. We handle all redirects so you don't lose your existing Google rankings during the transition.
Will my website work well on mobile phones? ▼
Every website we build is mobile-first, meaning we design for phones and tablets before scaling up to desktops. Google uses mobile-first indexing, so your mobile experience directly affects your search rankings. We test across iPhones, Android devices, and tablets to make sure everything works perfectly.
What platform do you build solicitor websites on? ▼
We use WordPress for most solicitor websites because it gives us full control over SEO, speed, and functionality. WordPress powers over 40% of all websites globally and is well-supported, secure (when properly maintained), and flexible enough to grow with your firm. We don't use drag-and-drop website builders that produce bloated, slow code.
