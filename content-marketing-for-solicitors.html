Why Content Marketing Matters for Solicitors

Google wants to show searchers the most helpful, trustworthy results. For legal topics, that bar is even higher. Well-written content is how your firm clears it.

E-E-A-T and Your Firm's Authority

Google evaluates legal websites using a framework called E-E-A-T: Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness. Legal services fall under what Google calls "Your Money or Your Life" (YMYL) topics, meaning the quality standard is stricter than for most industries.

What does that mean in practice? Your website needs to demonstrate that real solicitors with real qualifications are behind the advice. Thin pages with a paragraph of generic text will not rank. Google wants depth, accuracy, and evidence of genuine legal knowledge.

The firms that invest in quality content are the ones Google rewards with visibility. Every practice area page, blog post, and FAQ you publish is an opportunity to prove your firm's expertise to both Google and potential clients.

Topical Authority Wins Rankings

Topical authority means covering a subject area thoroughly enough that Google sees your site as the go-to resource. A single page about "family law" is not enough. You need pages covering separation, divorce, custody, maintenance, prenuptial agreements, and more.

When your site covers a practice area from every angle, Google starts ranking all of those pages higher. Each piece of content supports the others. A blog post answering "How long does a divorce take in Ireland?" reinforces the authority of your main divorce solicitor page.

  • Practice area pages build your core service visibility
  • Blog posts answer the questions clients search before they call
  • FAQ pages capture long-tail queries your competitors miss
  • Location pages connect your services to specific cities and counties
  • Legal guides position your firm as a trusted resource

LSRA Compliance in Legal Content

Publishing content for a solicitor's website is not the same as writing for any other business. There are rules, and getting them wrong can cause real problems.

Why This Matters

The Legal Services Regulatory Authority (LSRA) sets advertising and marketing guidelines that all solicitors in Ireland must follow. This includes the content published on your website. Claims about results, client testimonials, comparisons with other firms, and certain language around legal outcomes all need to meet LSRA standards.

Many generic content agencies are not aware of these rules. They produce content that could put your firm at risk. We build LSRA compliance into every stage of our writing process. Our team knows what can and cannot be said on a solicitor's website, and we work with your firm to ensure every page meets both regulatory requirements and your own professional standards.

This is one of the key reasons solicitors choose a specialist legal marketing agency over a generalist. The cost of getting it wrong is too high.

Content Marketing FAQs for Solicitors

How often should a solicitor's website publish new content? ▼
For most firms, two to four new pieces of content per month is a good starting point. This could be a mix of blog posts, FAQ updates, and new practice area pages. Consistency matters more than volume. Publishing one well-researched article per week will outperform publishing ten rushed pieces in a single month. We tailor the content calendar to your firm's capacity and goals.
Do you write the content or do our solicitors need to? ▼
We handle the writing. Our team researches and drafts every piece, then sends it to your firm for review. Your solicitors check the content for legal accuracy and approve it before publication. This keeps your team's time commitment to a minimum while ensuring everything published under your firm's name is accurate and appropriate.
How long does it take for content to start ranking on Google? ▼
New content typically starts appearing in search results within a few weeks of being indexed. However, reaching page one for competitive terms usually takes three to six months. Less competitive, long-tail keywords can rank faster. The key is consistency. Firms that publish regularly build topical authority faster, which lifts the rankings of all their pages over time.
What is the difference between content marketing and SEO? ▼
Content marketing is one part of a broader SEO strategy. SEO also includes technical work (site speed, mobile performance, crawlability), local SEO (Google Business Profile, citations), and link building. Content marketing focuses on creating the pages that rank. Without good content, there is nothing for Google to show in search results. The two work together, and we often deliver both as part of the same engagement.
Can you update our existing content or do you only write new pages? ▼
Both. In many cases, updating and expanding existing pages delivers faster results than creating new ones from scratch. If your site already has practice area pages or blog posts, we audit them first. Pages with some existing authority can often be improved and re-optimised to rank significantly higher. We will recommend the right mix of new content and updates based on what your site needs.
Is content marketing worth it for a small firm outside Dublin? ▼
Absolutely. In fact, smaller firms outside Dublin often see results faster because there is less competition in local search. A solicitor in Galway, Limerick, or Waterford can dominate local search results with a well-planned content strategy, often outranking larger firms that have not invested in content. Location-specific pages and locally relevant blog posts are particularly effective for firms serving regional markets.
